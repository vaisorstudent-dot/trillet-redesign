# Trillet — Strategic Pattern Library

*Extracted: 2026-02-28. Patterns identified across architecture, competitive landscape, traction data, and team analysis.*
*Purpose: Inform VC narrative, branding, GTM, and product positioning across all workstreams.*

---

## 1. Platform of Platforms

**Pattern:** Shopify / Stripe Connect
**Insight:** Trillet doesn't just serve agencies — it enables agencies to become SaaS companies. Each tier is a matryoshka doll of platform enabling.

| Tier | What the customer IS |
|------|---------------------|
| Starter | A user |
| Agency | A reseller |
| Lite SaaS | A SaaS company |
| Pro SaaS | An infrastructure operator |

**Use in VC narrative:** Name the pattern — investors recognize it instantly.
**Use in branding:** "Where agencies become SaaS companies."
**Use in GTM:** Sell the aspiration, not the feature. Agencies want to BE SaaS companies, not just use SaaS tools.

---

## 2. Infrastructure Becomes Invisible

**Pattern:** Stripe / Twilio
**Insight:** The more invisible Trillet becomes, the stickier it gets.

- D2C: Brand visible ("Trillet answers your calls")
- Agency: White-labeled (invisible to end clients)
- Pro SaaS: API calls (invisible even to the operator)

Removal difficulty increases as visibility decreases. This is counter-intuitive but proven: Stripe and Twilio won by disappearing into the stack.

**Use in VC narrative:** "We win by disappearing into our customers' products."
**Use in branding:** Brand investment should focus entirely on agencies (the buyer). End consumers never see Trillet. Don't invest in consumer awareness.
**Use in GTM:** Premium, infrastructure-grade brand feel (Stripe-clean, not GHL-busy).

## 3. Product IS Distribution

**Pattern:** GHL agency model / Shopify Partners
**Insight:** Every agency is a sales channel. Every template multiplies distribution. Each tier is an order of magnitude more distribution per customer.

- 1 Starter = 1 endpoint
- 1 Agency = 10–50 endpoints
- 1 Lite SaaS = 100+ endpoints
- 1 Pro SaaS = 1,000+ endpoints

The pricing structure IS the distribution strategy. The product self-distributes through its architecture.

**Use in VC narrative:** "Our CAC on a Pro SaaS deal yields thousands of endpoints."
**Use in branding:** N/A (internal framing)
**Use in GTM:** Prioritize higher-tier deals for distribution efficiency, not just ACV.

## 4. Triple Revenue Compounding

**Pattern:** Unique to Trillet's model
**Insight:** Standard SaaS compounds in one dimension (more customers). Trillet compounds in three simultaneously:

1. **More agencies** → subscription growth
2. **More endpoints per agency** → usage growth (each agency adding clients)
3. **More minutes per endpoint** → deepening usage (businesses get busier)

Each dimension multiplies the others. This is why $500K → $3-5M ARR is exponential, not linear.

**Use in VC narrative:** Show the three dimensions visually. Model the compounding effect.
**Use in branding:** N/A (investor-only framing)
**Use in GTM:** Track all three metrics: agency count, endpoints/agency, minutes/endpoint.

## 5. Telephony Exclusivity (Winner-Take-All at Endpoint)

**Pattern:** Unique structural property of voice AI
**Insight:** You can A/B test email tools. You can run parallel chatbots. You CANNOT run two AI receptionists on the same phone number. Voice AI is inherently winner-take-all at the endpoint level.

Switching is all-or-nothing, not gradual. This is a structural property of telephony, not a business model choice.

**Use in VC narrative:** "Telephony exclusivity — once we're handling the calls, there's no room for a competitor alongside us."
**Use in branding:** "Once you're the voice, you ARE the business's front door."
**Use in GTM:** Speed to deployment = speed to lock-in. The 5-minute URL setup is a competitive weapon.

## 6. Last Mile Ownership

**Pattern:** Amazon logistics / physical retail
**Insight:** Email can be ignored. Ads can be blocked. Chats are optional. But when a customer CALLS, someone MUST answer. The phone is the last mile — the most critical touchpoint.

Trillet owns the last mile of customer communication.

**Use in VC narrative:** "We own the last mile of customer communication" — powerful one-liner.
**Use in branding:** Potential tagline: "Own the last mile."
**Use in GTM:** Position against competitors who handle less-critical channels.

## 7. Regulated Industry Beachhead

**Pattern:** Enterprise "top-down" credibility
**Insight:** Government (legal hotline) + financial services (debt collection) + European banking = the HARDEST verticals. Having deployed here first means:

- Trillet can move DOWN to easier verticals trivially
- Competitors must climb UP through compliance barriers
- Regulated references open every door below them

**A Verizon reference sells dentists. A dentist reference doesn't sell Verizon.**

**Use in VC narrative:** Lead with regulated verticals as proof of enterprise maturity.
**Use in branding:** Security/compliance messaging is an asset, not overhead.
**Use in GTM:** Use enterprise logos (anonymized) to sell agency tier. "If Fortune 500s trust us, so can your clients."

## 8. Founder-Market Fit as Market Timing Evidence

**Pattern:** Shopify founders / Stripe founders
**Insight:** Ming was Bland.ai's first partner — present at the birth of the voice AI agency ecosystem. He built a consultancy to 7-figure ARR, saw the gap, and chose to build the PLATFORM instead of continuing to grow a profitable AGENCY.

This is the Shopify origin: ran a store → tooling didn't exist → built the platform. Or Stripe: tried to accept payments → tooling was terrible → built the infrastructure.

**He's not predicting a category. He LIVED the gap.**

**Use in VC narrative:** Lead the origin story. This is the strongest founder-market fit signal.
**Use in branding:** The "built by operators" narrative — Trillet was built by people who deployed voice AI at scale and knew what was missing.
**Use in GTM:** Credibility with agencies: "We were an agency. We know what you need because we needed it too."

## 9. GHL's Voice Feature = Market Validation, Not Threat

**Pattern:** Microsoft adding Teams (validated Slack's market)
**Insight:** GHL launched AI Employee / Voice AI features. This validates demand but isn't competitive because:

- GHL bolted voice onto marketing automation — Trillet built voice-first
- GHL's voice is a feature; Trillet's voice is the product
- GHL can't auto-configure from URL (marketing automation has no single deployment artifact)
- GHL can't create operational lock-in (marketing tools are switchable; phone systems aren't)

"GHL adding voice AI is the strongest market validation we could ask for. They see the demand. But bolting voice onto CRM is like adding a chatbot to a spreadsheet."

**Use in VC narrative:** Pre-empt the "what about GHL?" question with this framing.
**Use in branding:** Don't mention GHL publicly. Use the parallel only in investor/strategic contexts.
**Use in GTM:** Target agencies who tried GHL's voice AI and found it basic. They're pre-qualified leads.

## 10. The Factory That Creates Factories

**Pattern:** Meta-platform / recursive value creation
**Insight:** The closing frame across all patterns:

- Bland, Vapi, Retell built mousetraps (individual voice AI tools)
- GHL built a factory for marketing mousetraps
- **Trillet built the factory for voice AI mousetraps — and the factory creates factories**

Agencies on Trillet don't just deploy voice AI. They become voice AI companies. The recursive nature is the real thesis.

**Use in VC narrative:** Closing frame — "We're not building a better AI. We're building the system that lets others build, deploy, and sell AI at every scale."
**Use in branding:** "The platform that creates platforms."
**Use in GTM:** Sell transformation, not tools. "Stop being an agency. Start being a SaaS company."

## Branding Synthesis

**Current tagline:** "The platform agencies use to build, brand, and sell AI voice agents."
→ Accurate, specific, clear. Works well for the agency audience.

**Alternative directions emerging from patterns:**
- "Own the last mile" — visceral, different, positional (Pattern 6)
- "Where agencies become SaaS companies" — aspirational, transformation-focused (Pattern 1)
- "The voice AI platform that sells itself" — nods to distribution-as-product (Pattern 3)
- "Built by operators, for operators" — credibility play (Pattern 8)
- "The platform that creates platforms" — meta, cerebral, investor-friendly (Pattern 10)

**Design direction:** Stripe-clean infrastructure aesthetic. Dark, premium, precise. Not flashy consumer tech, not busy agency-tool UI. Trillet is infrastructure — it should feel like infrastructure.
